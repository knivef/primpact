Why crypto buyers keep coming back
Anonymity at checkout
Crypto payments require no card details, no billing address, no banking credentials. For a significant share of digital service buyers, this is not a preference — it is a requirement. Merchants who offer it earn their repeat order.
Platform loyalty
Crypto-paying customers are disproportionately loyal to platforms that support their preferred payment method. Once the onramp is complete and the first order lands, the barrier to returning is near zero. Merchants who remove friction on order one own that customer for order two through ten.
Borderless by default
Traditional payment rails exclude a large share of global buyers through card declines, FX friction, and geographic restrictions. Crypto-paying customers span markets that card payments cannot reach — and they return at higher rates because the alternative does not exist for them.
Trust through transparency
Blockchain settlement is verifiable and final. Crypto-paying customers know exactly what left their wallet and when. That clarity builds trust in the merchant faster than any traditional checkout flow.
